Clarifications

Specific data doesn't necessitate pinpoint accuracy to produce reasonably precise results. A variation of 10 units related to other same-tiered units on the field won't generate a notable variance, particularly at lower tiers. Similarly, when it comes to other player levels, aligning them with your own level—especially in the context of lower tiers—will frequently prove adequate. The fields with a light blue label above them denote less crucial information, while those with white text hold significance in ensuring accuracy.

Tier refers to the tier of each and every champion considered within this simulator. Due to distinct pools for each tier, solely champions of the same tier impact the likelihood of rolling a specific champion. This restriction is why you can only roll for champions sharing the same tier concurrently.

When providing the count of champions not in the pool, ensure you exclude those you intend to roll for. These are specified within the Unit tabs.

The hardcoded limit for the number of rolls per simulation is set at 300. This limitation prevents overwhelming the server with excessive requests, ensuring stable performance. For instance, trying to roll for a 3-star tier 5 unit at level 7, with only a 1% chance of getting a tier 5 unit per roll, may result in a resource-intensive situation without providing meaningful insights.

About the Simulator

This Monte Carlo simulator takes into account all variables that influence the chances of obtaining a specific number of copies of up to 3 distinct units of the same tier. The simulator emulates the shops of other players based on their levels, then repeatedly simulates its own shop until it accumulates all the required units. During this process, the simulator purchases champions of the same tier, except when the board is full.

The simulator assumes that you have one instance of each unit you're seeking on the field and keeps track of all other units on the board. When you acquire 1, 3, or 9 copies, depending on the star level, the simulator discontinues the search for that unit and treats any additional copies as same-tiered units while pursuing the remaining ones. After each shop simulation, the simulator checks whether the total copies found along with any duplicators meet the criteria or if further rolling is necessary.

This process is repeated 20,000 times, and the resulting data is presented in the chart for analysis.
